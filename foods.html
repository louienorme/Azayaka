Sushi (寿司)

It is a traditional Japanese dish of prepared vinegared rice, usually with some sugar and salt, accompanying a variety of ingredients, such as seafood, often raw, and vegetables.

  • Chirashizushi (ちらし寿司) serves the rice in a bowl and tops it with a variety of raw fish and vegetable garnishes.
  • Inarizushi (稲荷寿司) is a pouch of fried tofu typically filled with sushi rice alone.
  • Narezushi (熟れ寿司, "matured sushi") is a traditional form of fermented sushi. Skinned and gutted fish are stuffed with salt, placed in a wooden barrel, doused with salt again, then weighed down with a heavy tsukemonoishi (pickling stone).
  • Nigirizushi (握り寿司, "hand-pressed sushi") consists of an oblong mound of sushi rice that the chef presses between the palms of the hands to form an oval-shaped ball, and a topping (the neta) draped over the ball.
  • Makizushi (巻き寿司, "rolled sushi"), norimaki (海苔巻き, "nori roll") or makimono (巻物, "variety of rolls") is a cylindrical piece, formed with the help of a bamboo mat known as a makisu (巻き簾).

Tempura (天ぷら)

It is a typical Japanese dish usually consisting of seafood, meat, and vegetables that have been battered and deep fried.

Ramen (ラーメン)

is a Japanese noodle soup. It consists of Chinese wheat noodles served in a meat or (occasionally) fish-based broth, often flavored with soy sauce or miso, and uses toppings such as sliced pork (叉焼, chāshū), nori (dried seaweed), menma, and scallions.

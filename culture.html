Japanese Culture

日本の文化 (Nihon'nobunka)

Photo by Ryutaro Tsukata, pexels.com

The Japanese People are mostly well-mannered individuals

Manners and customs are an important part of Japanese life. Citizes of Japan grow up picking up the subtleties of this unique culture as they go through life, respecting the invisible and varied societal rules.

Bowing

One may notice that japanese always bow everytime they say hello, goodbye, thank you or sorry. It is a term of respect, remorse, gratitude and greeting.

Taking off footwear

Traditionally, Japanese People took off their shoes when entering homes, partly to keep the inside of the building clean, but also as a sign of respect.
